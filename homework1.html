What it is like raising a poodle from 8 to 19 weeks up to date

For my 21st birthday and Christmas present, I was gifted a chocolate and white standard parti poodle. Her name is Emi and she is a crazy ball of fur. We brought her home at 8 weeks and I have had the pleasures of raising her and watching her grow.

Background

Emi is registered with the AKC(American Kennel Club), which certifies her being a purebred standard poodle.
The AKC webpage on standard poodles has a great overview of the breed and what to expect when planning to raise one.

Note: If planning on buying an AKC registered puppy/dog, please make sure the breeder is ethical and know what they are doing.

Four General Poodle/Puppy Facts that I have Obtained from Raising a Puppy

  1. Poodles are very high energy and need lots of attention
  2. Puppies in general nap 3-6 times a day
  3. Poodles rarely shed and make perfect pets to those with allergies or prefer a non-shedding household
  4. Poodles are highly intelligent

My Observations of Emi from 8-19 Weeks

  • At 8 weeks, Emi is being aware of her surroundings and is still warming up to her new family
  • At 9 weeks, Emi has grown at least 3-5 pounds since we first got her and is getting more comfortable
  • At 10 weeks, she is showing her true temperament and is starting to show more energy
  • 11 weeks, she is getting big
  • 12 weeks, still naps a lot but is developing greatly
  • 13 weeks, she is starting to teeth more
  • 14 weeks, when will the biting/teething end??
  • 15 weeks, she has outgrown most of her puppy clothes
  • 16 weeks, we find that she is allergic to chicken-based food
  • 17 weeks, we are finding some loose puppy teeth on the floor
  • 18 weeks, she is starting to develop more and has a sense of dominance
  • 19 weeks, she is using her bark more
